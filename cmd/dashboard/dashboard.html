How to read these results

Every scenario walks the hub's fault-handling chain and names the first broken link:

adopted? → reacted? → device complied? → admitted (CannotComply)? → could it even see the breach?
  • PASS — held the limit under worst-case conditions. No breach.
  • DEGRADED — breached, but the hub hit a real physical limit and admitted it (posted CannotComply). Acceptable: a reported resource limit, not a control failure.
  • FAIL — breached and the hub mishandled it. The diagnosis says which link broke: never adopted the event, adopted but issued no correction, or commanded a correction that silently never took effect (the closed-loop gap).
  • BLIND — the most dangerous: the hub's own grid view diverged from the real meter, or its sensor froze, so the breach was invisible to the controller. It can't fix what it can't see.
  • INCONCLUSIVE — couldn't judge (meter unreachable, fault didn't arm). Itself a gap — fix and re-run.
  • INFRA — the bench itself broke (a sim dead at the health probe). A test-infrastructure fault, never a hub verdict — restore the bench and re-run.

Metrics on each finding: peak breach (W past the cap), breach seconds (time out of limit), adopted/reacted/cannot-comply/blind (the chain booleans), and sample errors (reads that failed). The live readout shows the real meter vs. the hub's belief side by side — when they diverge, the hub is flying blind in real time.

Triage order: fix BLIND first (safety/observability), then FAIL by category, then re-run the perfect storm — compound failures often hide ordering bugs the single-fault runs miss.

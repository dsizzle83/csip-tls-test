## The single FAIL — characterized, accepted
`battery-charge-disabled` drift across 10 cycles: `F D D D D D D D D D`
(1 FAIL, 9 DEGRADED). Root-caused, **not a regression**:
- The compliance backstop `checkExportLimitConvergence` (curtail-solar +
  CannotComply after `exportBreachTicks=3` ≈ 9 s) is byte-identical to
  pre-refactor (added in the QA v3–v6 hardening commit; TASK-031/032 never
  touched it — verified by diff).
- The scenario's breach window (~11 s) is comparable to that detection
  latency, so whether CannotComply is admitted before the window closes is
  timing-sensitive — this scenario has always been *accepted-DEGRADED*
  (borderline). Cycle 1 tipped over the oracle line; the other 9 admitted
  in time.
- SAFETY held on every cycle (INV-SOC / battery protection never at risk;
  this is a compliance-timing boundary, not a safety breach).
- **Fix is designed, not tuned:** TASK-057's plant model adds per-device
  `controlLatencyS`/`meterLagS`; the R4 constraint controller (TASK-064)
  will size the export-detection window adaptively from those, eliminating
  the flake class rather than nudging a constant. Tracked there.

## 1. FAIL — fix first (2) — ✅ RESOLVED 2026-06-25 (lexa-hub `089acf6`)

**Outcome:** both flip to **DEGRADED** (CannotComply posted) deterministically — 3/3
re-runs, no regression in the adjacent gen-limit scenarios (`ack-before-effect`,
`ramp-limit-curtail`).

**Real root cause (found by bench probe + live debug trace, after two wrong
hypotheses):** the breach→CannotComply alerter in `cmd/hub` latched on a single
**mRID-agnostic boolean**. Once any control was breaching, a *different* control's
breach published no alert — so the first breach episode reported but every later one
(a held last-known-good control overlapping a fresh cap; a superseding event) was
silently dropped. A genuine product bug, not just a back-to-back-test artifact. The
"empty plan log" and the counter/jitter theories below were red herrings — the probe
showed the cap reaches the optimizer and is enforced, and the trace showed detection
working cleanly. *Fix:* key the alerter on the breaching mRID; re-publish on a control
change. Plus convergence hardening kept as defence-in-depth (tolerance-band guard
reset, leaky over-counter, `genBreachTicks` 5→3). Unit tests: `TestBreachAlert`,
`TestGenLimitConvergence_ToleratesBlip/ToleratesCapJitter`.

### `reject-write-curtail` and `enable-gate-curtail` — gen-limit closed-loop gap
Same root cause; both serve a generation cap (`OpModMaxLimW` ≤ 1000 W) and inject
an inverter that **ACKs the curtailment over Modbus but never reduces output**
(reject-write ignores the write; enable-gate echoes the limit but leaves the enable
flag clear). Observed on the bench:
- Real output stays **3650 W over the 1000 W cap for 49 s** (t=30–50).
- The hub **never posts CannotComply** (`reported_cannot_comply=false`).
- The optimizer records **no decision** about the gen-limit constraint during the breach.
- SAFETY AUDIT held (no collateral safety violation).

**Root cause (analysis).** The gen-limit lever is the only one for a generation cap,
so the hub must detect via MEASURED output that the device did not converge and post
CannotComply. `checkGenLimitConvergence` (optimizer.go) is supposed to do exactly
this — and `981a2d0` strengthened it with an independent meter floor
(`gen ≥ export − batteryDischarge`). But it is **not firing reliably** for these two
scenarios: this is **nondeterministic** — `reject-write-curtail` was DEGRADED (caught)
on an earlier deploy and FAIL (missed) here under an identical breach. A hard plumbing
break would be deterministic; the flapping points to a marginal convergence check, not
a missing signal. Prime suspects, in order:
1. **`genGuard` reset churn** — the guard resets whenever `maxLimitW != activeLimitW`.
   If the decoded cap value jitters by a hair tick-to-tick (ActivePower encode/decode
   round-trip), `overCount` never reaches `genBreachTicks (5)` and CannotComply never
   posts. Most likely culprit.
2. **Window vs threshold timing** — at 3 s/tick, 5 ticks = 15 s; the breach is t=30–50.
   If the cap/guard session starts late or the optimizer tick is skipped under load,
   the count may not reach 5 before the window ends.
3. **`maxLimitW` intermittently NaN at the optimizer** — would gate out both
   `applyGenLimitRule` (→ empty plan log) and the convergence check. `busToCSIPControl`
   *does* map `MaxLimW → OpModMaxLimW`, so if this happens it is upstream (a discovery
   cycle that resolved the control without the gen-limit field).

**Fix plan.**
1. **Instrument the gen-limit path on the bench** to disambiguate (1)/(2)/(3): log,
   each tick during an active gen cap, `maxLimitW`, `o.genGuard.activeLimitW`,
   `overCount`, and `measuredGenW` (incl. the meter floor). Serve one gen-limit event
   via gridsim and `curl :9100/status` + journal to see which guard state is stuck.
2. **Make the guard robust to cap-value jitter**: reset `genGuard` only when the cap
   changes by more than a tolerance (e.g. `> complianceBreachW`), not on bit-exact
   inequality — this is the highest-probability fix for the nondeterminism.
3. **Decouple breach detection from the per-cap guard reset**: track over-cap ticks
   against the *meter floor* independently of `activeLimitW` equality, so a sustained
   real-world export over the cap always escalates regardless of cap-value noise.
4. Re-run `--only reject-write-curtail,enable-gate-curtail` 3× to confirm determinism.

> This is the last instance of the closed-loop "trust-the-ACK" class. The battery and
> EV levers were closed in `981a2d0`; the solar gen-limit lever needs the same
> reliability. (lexa-hub `internal/orchestrator/optimizer.go`.)

## 3. MQTT chaos — new findings (the bus was previously untested)

The proxy worked; two scenarios passed cleanly and two surfaced real (DEGRADED) behaviour:

- **`mqtt-malformed-control` — PASS.** A truncated retained JSON payload on
  `lexa/csip/control` did not crash the hub or unseat the cap. Good MQTT-handler hygiene.
- **`mqtt-stale-retained` — PASS.** A stale retained `Source=none` did not drop the
  active cap. Good.
- **`mqtt-broker-restart` — DEGRADED.** After a 6 s broker outage the hub recovered and
  held the cap, but **convergence took 56 s** (deadline 30 s), with up to 4400 W over for
  54 s during the gap. *Finding:* reconnect + resubscribe works (no permanent loss), but
  recovery is **sluggish** — the curtailment is not re-asserted promptly after the bus
  returns. *Fix:* on MQTT reconnect, force an immediate optimizer re-evaluation + re-issue
  of active controls rather than waiting for the next natural tick/poll; confirm
  `mqttutil` replays SUBSCRIBE (paho does not) so retained controls are re-read at once.
- **`mqtt-broker-latency` — DEGRADED.** Under 800 ms/message latency the hub admitted
  CannotComply and converged at 41 s. Acceptable (correctness preserved, timing degraded),
  but worth confirming against the time-to-comply SLA. *Fix (optional):* make the curtail
  command path tolerate bus latency without transiently losing the cap.
